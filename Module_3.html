Connecting PHP to MySQL: The Right Way

The ANCIENT Way ‚ò†Ô∏è

mysql_connect()

You'll see this in old tutorials. **DO NOT USE IT.**

  • DEPRECATED: It doesn't even exist in modern PHP. Your code will just crash.
  • DANGEROUS: It's wide open to SQL Injection attacks, where hackers can steal or delete your whole database.

The MODERN Way ‚ú®

new PDO()

This is the correct, secure, and modern way to connect.

  • SECURE: It uses "Prepared Statements" that 100% prevent SQL Injection.
  • MODERN: This is the standard. It's what all professionals and modern tutorials use.

What is phpMyAdmin?

"Easy Mode"

It's just a website that gives you buttons and forms to manage your database. When you click "Create Table," it just writes and runs the `CREATE TABLE` SQL command *for you*. It's perfect for setup, testing, and seeing your data visually.
